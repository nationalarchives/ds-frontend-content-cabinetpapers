Anthony Eden 1955

Conservative Government
5 April 1955 - 9 January 1957

When Churchill finally stood down a general election was called. The Conservatives were returned to power with 345 seats. Labour held 277 and the Liberals only six. However, the Suez Crisis soon overwhelmed the new Prime Minister, Anthony Eden. The crisis demonstrated the relative decline of British power, casting a long shadow over British foreign policy, and specifically over relations with the Arab world.
